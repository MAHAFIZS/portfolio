Thesis Title: Design and Implementation of Non-Contact Ballistocardiogram Signal Measurement in Bed and Disease Detection from BCG Signal

Thesis Grade: 1.0

Abstract: Heart’s valvular disease is one of the major concerns in modern healthcare. While ECG is the standard method for cardiac monitoring, its invasive nature can be uncomfortable for long-term use. This research proposed a non-invasive approach for detecting cardiac performance using Ballistocardiogram (BCG) signals captured through a piezoelectric sensor array embedded in a bed. The device captures the ballistic forces of the heart and compares them with conventional ECG results.

BCG data were collected from two local hospitals, maintaining all ethical guidelines. Artificial Neural Network (ANN) and Support Vector Machine (SVM) classifiers were used to analyze and detect valvular diseases — particularly Mitral Valve Stenosis and Pulmonary Valve Stenosis. The model's performance was evaluated based on overall accuracy, misclassification rate, and fitness. Results show promising potential for comfortable and continuous heart monitoring using non-contact methods.
